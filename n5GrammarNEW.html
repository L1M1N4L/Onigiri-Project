Chapter 1: Greetings and Introductions

Learn basic greetings and self-introductions to start conversations.

Key Phrases:

  • こんにちは (Konnichiwa) - Hello
  • おはようございます (Ohayou gozaimasu) - Good morning
  • こんばんは (Konbanwa) - Good evening
  • 私は [Your Name] です (Watashi wa [Your Name] desu) - I am [Your Name]
  • どうぞよろしくお願いします (Douzo yoroshiku onegaishimasu) - Nice to meet you

Practice Sentences:

  • あなたの名前は何ですか？ (Anata no namae wa nan desu ka?) - What is your name?
  • 初めまして、[Your Name] と申します (Hajimemashite, [Your Name] to moushimasu) - Nice to meet you, my name is [Your Name].

Chapter 2: Numbers and Time

Master counting objects and telling time in Japanese.

Counting Objects:

  • 一つ (Hitotsu) - One
  • 二つ (Futatsu) - Two
  • 三つ (Mittsu) - Three
  • 十 (Juu) - Ten
  • 百 (Hyaku) - One hundred

Telling Time:

  • 今、何時ですか？ (Ima, nan-ji desu ka?) - What time is it now?
  • 午前9時半 (Gozen ku-ji han) - 9:30 AM
  • 午後3時 (Gogo san-ji) - 3:00 PM

Practice Sentences:

  • 昼ご飯は何時ですか？ (Hirugohan wa nan-ji desu ka?) - What time is lunch?
  • 先週、日本に行きました。 (Senshuu, Nihon ni ikimashita) - Last week, I went to Japan.

Basic Sentences Practice

Practice constructing basic sentences in Japanese.

Example Sentences:

  • これはペンです (Kore wa pen desu) - This is a pen.
  • 私の趣味は音楽を聴くことです (Watashi no shumi wa ongaku o kiku koto desu) - My hobby is listening to music.
  • 日本の料理が好きです (Nihon no ryouri ga suki desu) - I like Japanese food.

Practice Sentences:

  • 新しい友達に会いました (Atarashii tomodachi ni aimashita) - I met a new friend.
  • 今週末、映画を見に行きます (Konshuumatsu, eiga o mi ni ikimasu) - This weekend, I will go to see a movie.

Chapter 3: Basic Vocabulary

Learn essential vocabulary for everyday communication.

Common Words:

  • 家 (ie) - House
  • 食べ物 (tabemono) - Food
  • 動物 (doubutsu) - Animal

Useful Phrases:

  • これは何ですか？ (Kore wa nan desu ka?) - What is this?
  • どうしてですか？ (Doushite desu ka?) - Why?

Chapter 4: Common Expressions

Explore common expressions used in various situations.

Everyday Phrases:

  • お疲れ様でした (Otsukaresama deshita) - Thank you for your hard work.
  • お願いします (Onegaishimasu) - Please/Kindly.
  • 失礼いたします (Shitsurei itashimasu) - Excuse me/Goodbye.

Politeness in Speech:

  • 敬語 (Keigo) - Polite language in Japanese.
  • 尊敬語 (Sonkeigo) - Respectful language.
  • 謙譲語 (Kenjougo) - Humble language.

Chapter 5: Cultural Insights

Gain insights into Japanese culture and customs.

Traditional Celebrations:

  • お花見 (Ohanami) - Cherry blossom viewing in spring.
  • お正月 (Oshogatsu) - New Year celebrations, including traditions and customs.

Etiquette and Politeness:

  • 礼儀正しい (Reigi tadashii) - The importance of politeness in Japanese culture.
  • 靴の脱ぎ履き (Kutsu no nugihaki) - Custom of taking off shoes when entering a home.
  • お辞儀 (Ojigi) - Bowing as a form of greeting and respect.

Chapter 6: Travel Phrases

Useful phrases for traveling in Japan.

Travel Situations:

  • ホテルでのチェックイン (Hoteru de no chekkuin) - Hotel check-in
  • レストランでの注文 (Resutoran de no chuumon) - Ordering at a restaurant
  • 駅での案内 (Eki de no annai) - Asking for directions at a station
