Japanese Writing Systems

Types of Letters in Japanese

  1. Hiragana: Phonetic sounds, used for particles, words, and parts of words.
  2. Katakana: Phonetic sounds, used for foreign/loan words.
  3. Kanji: Chinese characters, used for the stem of words, conveying meaning and sound.

About Hiragana

Hiragana is used to express 46 different sounds in the Japanese language. It is recommended to start learning Hiragana, followed by Katakana and then Kanji. Learning Hiragana first makes it easier to learn Katakana. Hiragana helps in proper Japanese pronunciation, reading beginners' textbooks, and writing sentences in Japanese. Mastery of Hiragana makes studying Japanese more accessible and facilitates writing emails in Hiragana.

About Katakana

Katakana is helpful for reading Japanese menus at restaurants. Both Hiragana and Katakana support Japanese study and comfortable living in Japan.

About Kanji

Kanji is a set of special characters used in Japanese writing. Each character stands for a word or a part of a word. They work together with hiragana and katakana, two other writing styles in Japanese. If you want to learn more about kanji, we have a separate module to learn more about kanji!

Furigana

Furigana is a Japanese reading aid, consisting of smaller-sized kana or syllabic characters, printed above or alongside kanji characters. It is used to help learners or readers who may not be familiar with the kanji characters or to indicate the correct pronunciation of uncommon or difficult words.

Mastering Hiragana

To master Hiragana, practice writing it regularly. Revision is crucial, so go over what you've learned several times.
